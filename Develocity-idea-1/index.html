01 — Why now

The human was always the bottleneck of software delivery. AI moved it.

For thirty years, software delivery pipelines were designed around a constant: the human. Build times, test cycles, review queues, deploy windows — every piece of the system was tuned to the cadence of developers typing, thinking, and approving. The pipeline was never designed to be the bottleneck. It didn't have to be.

AI changed the constant. Agents write at machine pace, produce volume no review queue was sized for, and read codebases no single engineer holds in their head. The pipeline — unchanged — is now the slowest thing in the loop. Five roles the human used to own are no longer reliably human.

01
Pacing
Feedback latency designed for human typing speed becomes a hard ceiling on agent effectiveness.
→ Scale
02
Gating
Review and approval choke points designed for human change volume stop choking AI-generated change.
→ Govern
03

No single engineer holds the mental model of an AI-generated codebase. Implicit context disappears.
→ Enable
04

Dependencies, toolchain components, and architectural patterns get chosen by training-data heuristics, not traceable human intent.
→ Govern
05
Consuming
Agents are first-class consumers of the toolchain. They need machine-native interfaces, not human-readable dashboards.
→ Enable

Develocity is the platform built for the new constraint.

02 — The wedge

Context engineering, not just observability.

Most observability platforms collect. Develocity encodes. The platform ingests the full telemetry of the software delivery pipeline — every build, every test, every dependency, every artifact, across every toolchain — and turns it into structured, semantically rich context that humans and AI agents can reason over. This is the context engineering layer.

Moat 01 — Data
The telemetry no one else has.

Build Scan is the observability primitive for the SDLC. It captures build, test, and dependency telemetry at a granularity no competitor collects, across every toolchain the enterprise runs on — Gradle, Maven, Bazel, npm, sbt, Python, and the CI systems, IDEs, and artifact repositories that connect to them. A raw data advantage that cannot be back-filled by ingesting more logs.

Moat 02 — Experience
A decade of encoded build expertise.

Raw telemetry is not context. Context is telemetry plus the semantic layer that makes it queryable — the encoded knowledge of how builds fail, how tests flake, how dependencies drift, how toolchains misbehave at scale. This expertise comes from the team that created Gradle Build Tool — over 70 million downloads a month — and a decade of diagnosing build systems inside the world's largest engineering organizations. The expertise is the product.

Neither moat is sufficient on its own. Together, they are the wedge.

Pillar 02 — Security budget

Govern.

Govern AI-generated software with end-to-end lineage and policy.

AI doesn't just write code — it writes dependencies, picks versions, pulls artifacts, and makes architectural decisions that used to be human calls. The security question is no longer "did the developer introduce a vulnerability?" It is "what did the agent build, where did every piece come from, and does it meet policy?" Develocity captures build-time provenance and dependency lineage across the entire toolchain, and enforces policy at the artifact layer — where AI-generated software actually ships. Pillar 03 — Engineering + Infra budgets

Scale.

Scale AI-driven build and test — fast and cost-efficient.
